TOUR DESCRIPTION

"A walk throught the most charming and historic squares of Granada". Sign up by filling out our form and we will contact you.

Plaza Bib-Rambla: The Arabic name of the square itself gives us a clue to its origin: Bib-Rambla (Puerta del Arenal) refers to its proximity to the sandy banks of the Darro River, which in those times still flowed openly throughout the urban center of the medina.

Plaza del Campillo: A well-known and emblematic place that has adapted to the various changes that the city has undergone over time. In it is located a white marble fountain of the nineteenth century. In it was the Bib al Taibín door, opened in the Nasrid wall. So from its origin it was a place of passage of the citizens of the place. This urban space is located in the heart of the city, very close to Puerta Real and with an outlet in the Acera del Darro. It takes up the space between the Post Office building and the Carrera de la Virgen..

Plaza Maria Pineda: The Plaza de Mariana Pineda (emblematic heroine of the liberal and romantic Granada) is the central location of the journey through the tragic history of this granadina. It was located a few steps from the house of the Lorca family on the Acera del Casino. In fact, the poet watched the coming and going of the square from his room every day. Mora Guarnido describes the place as "A vulgar small square, the most vulgar of the small squares of Granada without perspectives and without grace and in the center a bad marble statue that pretends to represent the poor Marianita in the figure of a fatty matron blonde..."

Plaza de las Pasiegas: It arose in 1692 when the Colegio de San Miguel was demolished and was enlarged at the end of the 18th century when the Colegio de Santa Catalina disappeared. Both buildings formed a block occupying the space where the square is located today. In 1946 it was remodeled according to a project by the civil engineer José Pérez Pozuelo and the municipal architect Luis Álvarez Cienfuegos, which included the construction of the steps and the paving with large slabs of stone from Sierra Elvira, transformations that remain in place today.

Plaza de Santo Domingo: The monument to Fray Luis de Granada, was made in 1910 by Pablo Loyzaga, and moved to this place from the Plaza de Bibarrambla in 1940. The main front of the square is presided over by the facade of the parish church of Santo Domingo, originally belonging to the adjoining monastery of Santa Cruz la Real. The building was completed in the mid-sixteenth century, showing a characteristic entrance portico with semicircular arches on columns, geminate window on it and belfry on the right side.

Plaza de San Gregorio: Walking along the slope of San Gregorio is like walking through the history of a city with wars, miscegenation, music and multiple creeds. It is one of the gates of the Lower Albaicín and can be reached by crossing the streets of Calderería Nueva and Vieja, famous for being a small Arab souk with dozens of tea shops and souvenir stores. Once at the top, the Plaza de San Gregorio is guarded by a cistern with fresh water that will delight any walker. There stands a cloistered convent where today live Poor Clare nuns who pray 24 hours a day without interruption and without ever turning their backs to the altar. Curiously, this is one of the parishes with more masses open to the public in Granada, so seeing them immersed in prayer dressed entirely in white is easy. However, a good time to do so is at noon because that is when they sing the Angelus together.

Plaza de la Victoria: The Plaza de La Victoria is today a modern site in the Albaicín. It borders the Calle San Juan de Los Reyes, and the Ave Maria school. The space now occupied by the Plaza de La Victoria once belonged to the orchards that made up the Convent of La Victoria, which were formerly the Arab orchards called Horra Batayna.

Mirador de Carvajales: The Mirador de Carvajales is located in the lower Albaizín, due to its location between the Cuesta de San Gregorio and Calle San Juan de los Reyes, it is one of the most intimate and least visited viewpoints in the neighborhood.
