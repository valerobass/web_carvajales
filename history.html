HISTORY OF THE SQUARE

Since the 16th century, the site that now houses the viewpoint was an empty space surrounded by buildings. It wasn't until the 19th century, with the extensive urban reforms carried out in the city, that several squares were created in the Albaicín, including Carvajales Square. In the 1960s, the Carvajales Viewpoint took on the appearance it retains today. In 1961, the retaining wall was built to raise the square, the ground was paved, the trees providing shade to the square were replanted, and several stone benches were installed, recently renovated. Before these renovations, the square was at a much lower level. It was so enclosed by the surrounding houses that the immediate surroundings were hardly visible.

ORIGIN OF THE SQUARE´S NAME

The square and the viewpoint are named after one of the most influential noble families in Granada during the 15th and 16th centuries, the Carvajal family. The family's history dates back to the conquest of Granada, in which Don Alonso Sánchez de Carvajal and Don Juan de Carvajal played an active role. The Carvajal family lineage, who were lords of Jódar in Jaén, originated from Valencia de Don Juan in León. Don Alonso Sánchez de Carvajal participated in the War of Granada and served as the custodian of the castles of Moclín and Lanjarón. His descendants included Pedro Hernández de Carvajal, who became a Knight of the Twenty-Four, and Juan Hernández de Carvajal, who served as the custodian of the castle of Alhama. Their noble coat of arms can be seen at number 29 Carrera del Darro, which is the Carvajal Palace or House of the Counts of Arco.
