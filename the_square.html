CARVAJALES SQUARE AND VIEWPOINT

We find ourselves in front of this emblematic square in the neighborhood. Rectangular, with benches and trees inviting rest, the square is crossed by a small pool that refreshes the hot summer days and invites us to contemplate the Alhambra from a different perspective. The Carvajales Viewpoint is located in the lower Albaicín; due to its location between Cuesta de San Gregorio and Calle San Juan de los Reyes, it constitutes one of the most intimate and less visited viewpoints in the neighborhood. Far from the hustle and bustle of other viewpoints such as Mirador de San Nicolas, Mirador de San Cristóbal, or Mirador de San Miguel Alto, the Carvajales Viewpoint is elusive and mysterious. In fact, one of the characteristics of the Carvajales Viewpoint is that the space encourages a calm gaze. It is a place where you can take a break, chat, read, or simply listen to music

ONE LOOK AT THE ANCIENT ALHAMBRA

The perspective that we can see of the Alhambra from the Carvajales Viewpoint is very different from what we see from any other place. In this case, we can see an Alhambra predating the Reconquista of the Catholic Monarchs. If we look closely, we can realize that from this perspective, none of the later modifications made during Christian times, such as the Palace of Charles V or the tower of the church of Santa Maria de la Alhambra, are visible. Therefore, we could say that it is an Alhambra from the Arab era, an original Alhambra.
