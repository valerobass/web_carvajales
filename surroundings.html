AROUND THE SQUARE

Very close to the square, we find the Cuesta de Carvajales Palace House, a structure with great heritage value that was built in the 16th century. It consists of a courtyard house that has undergone multiple modifications since its construction, starting with a Moorish design and later incorporating a Castilian house style. Since the 20th century, this structure has served as a multi-family residence, due to the high demand for worker housing during those years. Thus, the large house that used to be inhabited by a single family now provides shelter to several. Pilar Placeta de Carvajales: On the lower level of the Carvajales Viewpoint, there is a pillar, almost forgotten today, which likely has been in the same spot since the 19th century, the date of the construction of the square. The structure is set on a Granada cobblestone floor. It consists of a large rectangular basin with straight lines and slightly rounded corners. The basin is made up of several blocks of Elvira stone joined together. It has a curved profile on the top, over 25 centimeters wide. This pillar does not have a proper pediment; this function is performed by the high wall that supports the square. A few centimeters above the edge of the basin, there are three Sierra Elvira stone plates, from which three simple water spouts emerge.
